# Goal guidance

openkoutsi can ask its AI coach, **Koutsi**, to look at one of your goals and tell
you two things: whether it is realistic for the timeline you set, and what to
actually do to reach it.

## Setting a goal

On the **Goals** page you can set training or event goals with an optional target
metric and value (for example, FTP 300 W) and a target date. Goals are yours to
track — see [Goals](index.md) for the basics.

## Asking Koutsi for guidance

Each active goal has a **Get AI guidance** button. When you press it, Koutsi looks
at the goal alongside your current fitness (CTL/ATL/TSB), your recent training, and
your active plan, and writes back a short piece of coaching advice.

The guidance is generated on demand — it is not refreshed automatically — so it
only runs when you ask for it. While Koutsi is thinking, the card shows a typing
indicator; the finished guidance is saved on the goal, so it is still there when
you come back.

## The realism verdict

Every piece of guidance opens with a colour-coded verdict:

- **Realistic** — the goal is well within reach on your current timeline and
  trajectory.
- **Ambitious** — a real stretch, but achievable with focused, consistent work.
- **Unrealistic** — not attainable without a change, such as extending the date,
  adjusting the target, or a big step up in training.

Below the verdict, Koutsi explains the reasoning in your athlete's numbers and
gives concrete steps to move toward the goal.

!!! tip "Re-run after changes"
    Guidance is a snapshot of the moment you asked. If you edit the goal or your
    fitness moves on, press **Refresh** to get an up-to-date read.

!!! info "Written in your language"
    The advice is written in your interface language. The verdict word itself
    (realistic / ambitious / unrealistic) drives the coloured badge.

!!! note "Requires AI to be available"
    Goal guidance uses an AI model. On servers where AI features require a
    subscription, you will be prompted to subscribe or to
    [connect your own model](using-your-own-ai-model.md).
